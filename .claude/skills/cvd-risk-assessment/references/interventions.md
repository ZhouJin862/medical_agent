# Intervention Recommendations by Risk Level

## Lifestyle Interventions (All Risk Levels)

### Dietary Recommendations

| Recommendation | Target | Evidence |
|----------------|--------|----------|
| Sodium restriction | <5g/day (2000mg Na+) | Reduces SBP 2-8 mmHg |
| DASH diet | Fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy | Reduces SBP 8-14 mmHg |
| Mediterranean diet | Plant-based, olive oil, nuts | 30% CVD risk reduction |
| Saturated fat | <7% of total calories | LDL reduction |
| Fiber | 25-40g/day | LDL reduction 5-10% |
| Fish | 2 servings/week | Omega-3 benefits |

### Physical Activity

| Level | Recommendation |
|-------|----------------|
| Basic | 150 min/week moderate intensity OR 75 min/week vigorous |
| Enhanced | 300 min/week moderate for additional benefit |
| Resistance | 2 sessions/week, major muscle groups |
| Note: For sedentary patients, start with 10-15 min/day and gradually increase |

### Weight Management

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| BMI | 18.5-23.9 (Asian optimal) |
| Waist | M<90cm, F<85cm |
| Weight loss | 5-10% for overweight/obese |

### Smoking Cessation

- **Immediate benefit**: CVD risk drops 50% within 1 year
- **Methods**: Counseling + pharmacotherapy (varenicline, NRT)
- **Follow-up**: Weekly for first month, then monthly

### Alcohol Limits

- Men: <25g/day (~2 standard drinks)
- Women: <15g/day (~1 standard drink)
- Abstinence recommended for hypertension or heart failure

## Medical Interventions by Risk Level

### Low Risk (低危)

| Treatment | Indication | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Antihypertensive | If BP ≥160/100 after lifestyle change | <140/90 mmHg |
| Statin | LDL-C ≥4.9 mmol/L | <3.4 mmol/L |
| Antiplatelet | Not routinely recommended | - |
| Follow-up | Annually | Reassess risk |

**Lifestyle focus**: Diet modification, exercise, weight control, smoking cessation

### Medium Risk (中危)

| Treatment | Indication | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Antihypertensive | If BP ≥140/90 after 3 months lifestyle | <140/90 mmHg |
| Statin | Consider if LDL-C ≥3.4 or multiple risk factors | <3.4 mmol/L |
| Antiplatelet | Individualize (consider if high bleeding risk) | - |
| Follow-up | Every 6 months | Monitor progress |

**Statin intensity**: Start moderate intensity if initiated

### High Risk (高危)

| Treatment | Indication | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Antihypertensive | Indicated for most | <130/80 mmHg |
| Statin | Recommended | <2.6 mmol/L (50% reduction) |
| Antiplatelet | Consider based on bleeding risk | - |
| Follow-up | Every 3-6 months | Close monitoring |

**Statin intensity**: Moderate to high intensity
**First-line agents**: ACEI/ARB, CCB, thiazide

### Very High Risk (很高危)

| Treatment | Indication | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Antihypertensive | Indicated | <130/80 mmHg |
| Statin | High intensity | <1.8 mmol/L (50% reduction) |
| Antiplatelet | Aspirin 75-100mg/day (if not contraindicated) | - |
| Beta-blocker | If post-MI or heart failure | - |
| ACEI/ARB | If diabetes, CKD, or LV dysfunction | - |
| Follow-up | Every 1-3 months | Intensive management |

**Statin intensity**: High intensity (atorvastatin 40-80mg, rosuvastatin 20-40mg)

## Target Organ Protection

### Diabetes
- HbA1c <7.0% (individualized)
- BP <130/80 mmHg
- LDL-C <1.8 mmol/L
- Annual: Retinal exam, foot exam, urine albumin

### CKD
- BP <130/80 mmHg
- ACEI/ARB preferred
- SGLT2 inhibitors if eGFR appropriate
- Monitor eGFR, K+ every 3 months

## Red Flags Requiring Immediate Referral

| Symptom | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Chest pain | Emergency evaluation |
| Dyspnea at rest | Urgent cardiology |
| Syncope | Cardiac workup |
| New-onset atrial fibrillation | Anticoagulation assessment |
| BP ≥180/120 with symptoms | Hypertensive emergency |

## Monitoring Schedule

| Risk Level | BP | Lipids | Glucose | Weight |
|------------|-----|--------|---------|--------|
| Low | Every 2 years | Every 4-5 years | Every 3 years | Annually |
| Medium | Annually | Every 2-3 years | Every 1-2 years | Every 6 months |
| High | Every 3-6 months | Every 6-12 months | Every 3-6 months | Every 3-6 months |
| Very High | Every 1-3 months | Every 3-6 months | Every 3 months | Every visit |

## Patient Education Priorities

### Understanding Your Risk
- Explain risk category in simple terms
- Discuss modifiable vs non-modifiable factors
- Set realistic expectations for improvement

### Medication Adherence
- Importance of daily compliance
- What to do about missed doses
- Side effect monitoring
- When to seek help

### Self-Monitoring
- Home BP monitoring technique
- Weight tracking
- Symptom diary for high-risk patients
- When to call the doctor

## Follow-up Template

**At each visit, review:**
1. Risk factor control (BP, lipids, glucose)
2. Medication adherence and side effects
3. Lifestyle goal achievement
4. New symptoms or concerns
5. Adjust treatment plan as needed
