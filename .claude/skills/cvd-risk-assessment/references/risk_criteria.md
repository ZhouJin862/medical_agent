# Cardiovascular Risk Criteria (Chinese Guidelines)

## Risk Stratification Algorithm

### Step 1: Check for Existing CVD
If patient has established cardiovascular disease → **Very High Risk** (Secondary Prevention)

Established CVD includes:
- Previous myocardial infarction
- Angina pectoris
- Coronary revascularization (PCI/CABG)
- Ischemic stroke
- Peripheral artery disease

### Step 2: Check for High-Risk Conditions
Automatic **Very High Risk** if:
- Diabetes with target organ damage (microalbuminuria, retinopathy, neuropathy)
- CKD stage 3-5 (eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m²)

Automatic **High Risk** if:
- Diabetes without target organ damage
- Single risk factor severe elevation:
  - LDL-C ≥4.9 mmol/L
  - TC ≥7.2 mmol/L
  - SBP ≥180 mmHg and/or DBP ≥110 mmHg

### Step 3: Count Major Risk Factors

| Risk Factor | Definition | Points |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| Age | Men ≥45 years, Women ≥55 years | 1 |
| Hypertension | SBP≥140 or DBP≥90, or on antihypertensive | 1 |
| Dyslipidemia | LDL-C≥3.4 or on lipid-lowering therapy | 1 |
| Smoking | Current smoker | 1 |
| Obesity | BMI≥28, or waist ≥90cm(M)/≥85cm(F) | 1 |
| Family History | Premature CVD (M<55, F<65) in 1st-degree relatives | 1 |

### Step 4: Determine Risk Category

| Risk Factor Count | Risk Category | 10-Year ASCVD Risk |
|-------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 0 | Low | <5% |
| 1-2 | Medium | 5%-9% |
| ≥3 | High | ≥10% |

*Note: If 10-year risk score available, use calculated value over factor count*

## China-PAR Risk Score Key Parameters

For more precise risk assessment, China-PAR equation considers:
- Age, gender
- Region (North/South China, Urban/Rural)
- Education level
- SBP, treatment of hypertension
- TC, HDL-C
- Smoking status
- Diabetes
- Family history of CVD
- BMI

**Thresholds for 10-year risk:**
- Low Risk: <5%
- Medium Risk: 5%-9%
- High Risk: ≥10%

## Special Populations

### Elderly (≥75 years)
- Individualize assessment based on functional status and life expectancy
- Focus on blood pressure control and symptom management

### Young Adults (<40 years)
- Assess lifetime risk in addition to 10-year risk
- Focus on risk factor modification and lifestyle

### Women
- Consider pregnancy history (preeclampsia, gestational diabetes)
- Menopause status affects risk calculation
